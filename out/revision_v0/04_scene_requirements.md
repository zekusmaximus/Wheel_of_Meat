## Moment 1: Devaka's Betrayal (SELECTED)

### Current Status: Summary/Tell

**Location**: Chapter 4 (Mauryan Chandra), Chapter 5 (Lilith CP02)

**Current text** (≤25 words):
> "The betrayal would proceed. The lesson would be taught." (Ch 5, line ~1895)

> "The last thing he saw before the darkness swallowed him was Devaka's face..." (Ch 4, line ~1434)

### Problem
We see Chandra's suspicions, we see him walking into the trap, we see his capture—but we never see Devaka actually deliver the information to the conspirators. The moment of betrayal itself is narrated in summary.

### Dramatization Spec

**Decision**: Devaka chooses to warn the conspirators

**Cost**: His friendship with Chandra, his self-image as a man of integrity

**Irreversible consequence**: Chandra's imprisonment and death become inevitable

**Sensory anchors**:
- Oil lamp flickering in the underground meeting room
- Sweat on Devaka's palms as he speaks
- The conspirators' faces—named individuals with positions, not abstractions
- The document he hands over, Chandra's handwriting visible
- His own voice sounding unfamiliar in his ears

**Scene structure** (~1,500 words):
1. Devaka approaching the conspirators' meeting (internal justification)
2. The moment of speaking—actually saying the words that condemn Chandra
3. The conspirators' response (relief? gratitude? contempt?)
4. Devaka's immediate aftermath—what he does/feels in the hours after

**Aftermath linkage into Manchester**:
Lilith's tracking passage (Ch 5) must show her observing THIS scene, not just its effects. She watches Devaka speak, watches his hands shake, watches the conspirators' faces harden into decision.

## Moment 2: Lilith's First Intervention (SELECTED)

### Current Status: Partially Dramatized but Unclear

**Location**: Chapter 5 (Lilith CP02), referenced throughout

**Current text** (≤25 words):
> "She felt her own presence inside the shift. Participant. Not witness." (Ch 5, line ~1893)

### Problem
We see Lilith affect Devaka's fear-thread, but the transition from observation to manipulation is subtle. The editor asks: "When does observation become intervention?" This moment should be pivotal.

### Dramatization Spec

**Decision**: Lilith chooses to influence rather than merely watch

**Cost**: Her identity as objective observer; the beginning of 300,000 years of "backfiring interventions"

**Irreversible consequence**: She becomes a participant in the wheel's turning; she can never claim neutrality again

**Sensory anchors**:
- Her flat in Manchester (grounding contrast)
- Physical sensation of reaching into the pattern (heat? pressure? cold?)
- The tea going cold beside her
- The specific moment her intention shifts from "observe fear" to "amplify fear"
- Her breath catching as she realizes what she's done

**Scene structure** (~1,200 words):
1. Lilith in tracking state, observing Devaka's emotional patterns
2. The temptation—seeing how easily fear could be nudged
3. The choice—the actual moment of reaching
4. The sensation of contact/influence
5. Immediate recoil/recognition of what she's become

**Aftermath linkage into Manchester**:
This scene establishes the pattern she will repeat for 300,000 years. The phrase "This intervention will work" should appear here for the first time, showing its origin as hope before it becomes desperate incantation.

## Moment 3: Stewart's Rooftop Fall (SELECTED)

### Current Status: Described but Compressed

**Location**: Chapter 22 (Contemporary Breakthrough)

**Current text** (≤25 words):
> "But the turn carried him. His foot found the barrier's base, and what had been a pivot became a stumble, and what had been a stumble became gravity..." (line ~365)

### Problem
The editor requests "one more sentence of suspended time" before gravity takes hold. The moment is pivotal—it catalyzes Silas's awakening and creates the chain with Lilith—but it happens too quickly for its weight.

### Dramatization Spec

**Decision**: Stewart's turn toward Silas (connection-seeking)

**Cost**: His balance, potentially his life

**Irreversible consequence**: The chain forms—Silas reaches, Lilith grabs, three become linked

**Sensory anchors**:
- The rain on the rooftop, wind pulling at clothes
- The barrier's metal edge under Stewart's hand
- The city lights of Manchester below
- Silas's voice saying Stewart's name
- The exact sensation of weight shifting past the point of recovery
- Time dilating in the fall

**Scene expansion** (+500-800 words):

Current beat (keep):
> "Tat tvam asi." Stewart's hand left the barrier.

**Add suspended time**:
- Stewart's turn in slow motion
- His foot finding the barrier's base
- The micro-moment of recognition that balance is lost
- His eyes meeting Silas's
- The Sanskrit phrase hanging incomplete in the air
- Gravity beginning to claim what balance has released

**Add Silas's lunge in detail**:
- The physical mechanics of reaching
- His own weight going forward over the barrier
- The moment when he should fall too
- Lilith's arms around his waist (grounding him)
- The chain completing

**Aftermath linkage into Manchester**:
The scene must land back in the physical—rain on faces, cold metal, Stewart's breathing, the city noise returning. The metaphysical chain is real but the rooftop is real too.

## Dramatization Quality Checklist

For each expanded scene, verify:

- [ ] **Decision is visible**: Reader sees the character choosing
- [ ] **Cost is felt**: Something is lost/sacrificed
- [ ] **Consequence is irreversible**: No take-backs possible
- [ ] **Sensory anchors present**: At least 3 physical details grounding the scene
- [ ] **Aftermath links to Manchester**: Scene connects forward to present-day frame
- [ ] **Subtext over recap**: Scene shows, then moves on (no explanatory summary after)
