## Scene Structure

### Scene 1: "The Method" (~1,200 words)

**Setting:** Lilith's flat, Manchester, early morning

**Function:** Establish the forward-tracking technique and her state after a year of intense work

**Beats:**
- Opens in tracking corner, surrounded by notebooks spanning eight years
- Physical cost visible: exhaustion lines, coffee cups, narrowed world
- Demonstrates forward-tracking technique: "dropping in" on deaths/transitions to map soul trajectory
- References the decision from Ch17: "No more intermediaries. Find him in the present."
- Has traced through several intermediate lives in the past year
- Today she'll observe the Taiping incarnation—the last major waypoint before present

**Physical Details:**
- Migraines from extended tracking
- The flat has become a command center, not a home
- Notebooks everywhere—a decade's work made physical

**Closes with:** Transition into tracking session

---

### Scene 2: "The Observation — Spring 1862" (~1,400 words)

**Setting:** Tracking observation of Wei Shufen, Taiping Rebellion

**Function:** Establish the Ch18 incarnation; show Lilith's reconnaissance mindset

**Beats:**
- She "drops in" on Wei in Spring 1862, Tianjing under siege
- Observes him: military medic, lüshuai rank, ledger-obsessed
- Notes his theological faith: believes in divine protection for those he saves
- Recognizes the determination (adhiṭṭhāna) perfection developing
- Sees it as contaminated: attached to outcomes, to divine reward
- Calculates: "If everyone he saves dies anyway, the determination will break"
- Decides to intervene while tracking—"efficiency"

**What she observes:**
- Wei treating wounded at dawn
- His meticulous ledger counting
- The genuine faith in his work
- Flaw spotted: outcome-dependent determination

**Closes with:** Decision to test her near-full manifestation powers

---

### Scene 3: "The Intervention" (~1,600 words)

**Setting:** Tracking observation spanning 1862-1864

**Function:** Show her manipulation over two years; demonstrate power level

**Beats:**
- Montage of circumstantial manipulation (not physical presence)
- Artillery timing adjusted → evacuees shelter where shells land
- Unit assignments influenced → healed soldiers sent to suicide missions
- Disease vectors guided → patients die of illness days after Wei saves them
- Intelligence corrupted → rescue attempts walk into ambushes
- Supply chains disrupted → medicine "lost" or delayed
- Shows the effort required: attentional strain, hours lost in modern timeline
- Her satisfaction as the pattern emerges: "Everyone he saves dies"

**The progression:**
- 1862: Isolated incidents, looks like bad luck
- 1863: Wei begins to sense the pattern—"Am I cursed?"
- Early 1864: Wei's faith fracturing; ledger numbers inverting
- June 1864: Hong Xiuquan dies; Wei stops helping

**Her assessment:** "It's working. He's breaking."

**Closes with:** Relief—she can move on to continue tracking forward

---

### Scene 4: "The Pause — June 1864" (~1,000 words)

**Setting:** Tracking observation, post-Hong's death

**Function:** Show what Lilith believes is victory

**Beats:**
- Hong Xiuquan dies (June 1, 1864)
- Wei sits among wounded, paralyzed
- Does not move to help for two days
- Lilith observes with satisfaction: "Finally. Determination broken."
- She begins preparing to track forward to the next incarnation
- Brief return to modern consciousness: coffee, stretching, notes

**Her internal assessment:**
- "Theology crumbled. Hope extinguished. Futility visible."
- "He has stopped. The perfection is corrupted."
- "I can trace forward now. Find the next thread."

**Closes with:** Return to observation to confirm—and sees something unexpected

---

### Scene 5: "The Backfire" (~1,800 words)

**Setting:** Tracking observation, June 1864

**Function:** CLIMAX — The backfire realized; her intervention purified rather than broke him

**Beats:**
- A wounded child appears before Wei
- Wei hesitates—remembering two years of everyone dying
- Then helps anyway
- Lilith's shock: "No. No, he's supposed to stop."
- Watches him continue through the siege, helping futilely but persistently
- The breakthrough visible: determination purified, not broken
- "He knows it's futile. He helps anyway. Not for God. Not for results. Because it's what he does."

**The realization:**
> "I broke his faith. I shattered his hope. I made futility visible.
> 
> And all I did was purify him."

**Lista of backfires surfaces:**
- Ch5: Betrayal taught generosity without reciprocity
- Ch7: Isolation concentrated rather than diluted insight
- Ch9: Exile became space for transformation
- Ch11: Formalization became mirror
- Ch13: Stasis field taught impermanence through horror
- Ch15: Persecution became crucible for releasing hatred
- Ch17: Doubt sharpened into truthfulness
- Ch19: Futility purified determination from outcome-attachment

**Her frustration:**
> "Even when I'm not trying to help him, I help him. Even my reconnaissance accelerates his path."

**Closes with:** Resignation—continue tracking forward despite the failure

---

### Scene 6: "The Death" (~1,500 words)

**Setting:** Tracking observation, July 19, 1864

**Function:** Witness Wei's death; plant Maitreya foreshadowing

**Beats:**
- Walls breach, Qing forces flood in
- Wei organizes futile evacuation (Lilith's cavalry intercepts it)
- Stays with wounded who cannot flee
- Dies protecting them from Qing soldiers
- Final thought echoes in her awareness: "I saved no one. But the trying was not nothing. I will try again."

**Maitreya foreshadowing:**
- As she watches him die, inexplicable chest pain
- Not physical—something deeper
- She dismisses it: "Attentional strain. Nothing more."
- But the reader sees: empathic connection she doesn't recognize

**Her processing:**
- Adds another backfire to the ledger
- Notes the pattern: "Opposition accelerates. Distance fails."
- The strategic conclusion crystallizes

**Closes with:** Following the soul forward through the rebirth process
