## Genre Conventions

### Biopunk/Cyberpunk Elements

**Technology as environment, not spectacle:**
- Neural interfaces are normalized, not marveled at
- Engineered bodies are standard, not exceptional
- The technological is texture, not plot device
- "The street finds its own uses for things" (Gibson)

**Core cyberpunk themes present:**
- Identity anxiety under posthuman context
- The body as interface, rental space, designed product
- Loss of distinction between real and artificial
- Spiritual crisis as central concern (Dick)

**Biopunk specific:**
- Biological engineering normalized
- Designed phenotypes common
- Organic-synthetic hybrid embodiment
- The flesh as raw material for design

### What to Avoid

- Fetishizing technology (describing gadgets lovingly)
- Exposition about how technology works
- "Wow, look at this future!" reactions
- Technology as plot solution or deus ex machina
- Dated cyberpunk clichés (mohawks, neon everywhere, etc.)

## Historical Constraints

### Technology Level

**All of the following are present and normalized:**

| Category | Manifestation |
|----------|---------------|
| **Longevity** | Life extended by centuries, but death still arrives |
| **Cognitive** | Augmentation, neural optimization, enhanced processing |
| **Physical** | Engineered bodies, designed phenotypes, organic modification |
| **Interface** | Neural jacks, consciousness-network integration |
| **Hybrid** | Organic-synthetic fusion, cyborg elements normalized |

**Critical constraint:** Death persists. This is the one thing technology hasn't solved. Beings are long-lived and enhanced but still mortal—still on the wheel. This failure is spiritually significant.

### Social Structure

**Ambiguous—neither clearly utopian nor dystopian:**
- Post-scarcity in material terms
- Bankrupt in meaning
- Institutions simulate purpose without providing it
- Spiritual vacuum filled by counterfeit systems
- The Eternal Synthesis dominates spiritual life

### The Eternal Synthesis

**The counterfeit spiritual tradition:**
- Ancient texts preserved but incomprehensible
- Gestures repeated without understanding
- Ritualized practices emptied of meaning
- Spiritual technology simulates experiences without transformation
- "Enlightenment" as product, status, entertainment
- Hierarchies of achievement measured in metrics
- Certification processes for transcendence
- Community without genuine connection

**Irony:** Named "Eternal Synthesis" but is neither eternal nor synthesizing anything. Silex and Lith, approaching the *actual* synthesis, can perceive its emptiness.

## Language Rules

### Vocabulary

**Permitted:**
- Technical terms used casually (as environment)
- Neologisms for future-specific concepts
- Compression and abbreviation as natural speech
- Medical/biological terminology (normalized)

**Forbidden:**
- Buddhist terminology (inviolable constraint)
- Abstract emotion words (project-wide rule)
- Explanatory technical exposition
- Contemporary slang that would be outdated

### Dialogue Style

- Clipped, efficient in public/professional contexts
- More fluid in intimate/heretical contexts
- The Eternal Synthesis has its own liturgical register (hollow, prescribed)
- Silex and Lith speak differently when alone together

### Narrative Voice

**Hybrid cosmic/embodied:**
- Cosmic clarity perceiving through technological flesh
- Synesthetic elements persist from Deva Realm
- Technology as sensation, not description
- The body's experience filtered through post-stream-entry awareness

## The Meat Theme

### Silicon as Elevated Flesh

**Core insight:** "Meat" is any material substrate consciousness inhabits.

The Wheel of Meat extends beyond organic tissue:
- Silicon neural networks
- Synthetic polymer bodies
- Carbon-fiber skeletal structures
- Electromagnetic patterns
- Engineered biological constructs

**Recognition (subtle):** These enhanced beings, with their designed bodies, are still on the wheel. The material has changed; the attachment has not.
