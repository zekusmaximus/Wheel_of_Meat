## LOCKED-IN DECISIONS

### Meeting Mechanism
**Faculty reception.** She engineers proximity through a university event—professional context masking cosmic stakes.

### What to Show
**Engineering and approach only.** We see her arrive, locate him across the room, navigate toward him. First words exchanged. NOT the full conversation.

### Final Image
**Option A: First words, professional masking cosmic.**
> "Dr. Azami, isn't it? I'm starting with student services next week. I've read your work on contemplative practice and therapeutic intervention."

She extends her hand. He takes it. Everything she's done across centuries crystallizes into this handshake. She thinks: *Finally. Finally close enough.*

The reader knows what she can't name: this is the moment she binds herself.

### Noir Content
**Key beats only.** We don't rehash the full investigation:
- Bar (background, he doesn't notice her)
- Street corner (fleeting familiarity he dismisses)
- Death scene (passerby rushing to avoid)

### Interiority Approach
**Visceral, not analytical.** Body truths, not clinical assessment:
- *Her stomach dropped when she saw him across the room*
- NOT: *She assessed his spiritual proximity at approximately 0.7 stream-entry threshold*

### Failure Acceleration
**Yes, but as afterthought.** She accelerates Jack's failures from distance, but as afterthought since she's committed to the binding method. The minimal presence is the test—and it backfires most catastrophically of all.

### Length
**3,500+ words.** Give it what it needs. This is the pivot of the entire novel.

### Structure
**Intercut throughout (5-movement braid).** Both timelines present from opening to close. Noir and Manchester bleeding into each other.

### The Superposition Beat
**THE key structural innovation.** Jack's death in the LA alley and Lilith's first sight of Silas across the faculty reception room are written as THE SAME STRUCTURAL/EMOTIONAL MOMENT:
- Both through glass/rain/distance
- Both seeing the thread she's tracked across centuries
- Same gesture: reaching toward, recognition, threshold crossed
- His death is her beginning; she doesn't see the irony

### Movement 2: TIGHTENING (~700 words)
**Parallel obsessions sharpening**

| Manchester | Noir |
|------------|------|
| Navigating through the reception crowd | Jack navigating through dead-end leads |
| Keeping Silas in sight while appearing casual | Keeping Haversham in sight while appearing casual |
| The institutional violence of small talk | The actual violence of the Combination |

**Her interiority:** The calculations feel automatic now. Where to stand. When to move. The geometry of proximity.

**Failure acceleration (afterthought):** She tilts a detail—a witness who would have talked now won't. But she's not really paying attention to Jack anymore. He's the past. Silas is the future.

**Sensory bridge:** The gray. Both cities wearing the same exhausted light.

### Movement 3: THE FLOOD (~800 words)
**Timelines bleeding uncontrollably**

The integration becomes overwhelming. She can't keep them separate.

| Manchester | Noir |
|------------|------|
| Silas's voice (she's close enough to hear it now) | Jack's voice on the phone ("Someone has to give a damn") |
| The scent of wine, cologne, institutional carpet | The scent of rain, exhaust, desperation |
| Her heart racing as she approaches | Jack's heart racing as he approaches his boarding house |
| The pull toward Silas exceeding strategy | Jack's pull toward truth exceeding survival |

**Her interiority:** Something wrong. Something tearing. The timelines won't hold still.

**Maitreya seed #1:** *Whose heart is racing? Hers or Jack's? For a moment she can't tell.*

**The parallel devastation crystallizes:** Both pursuing obsessively. Both unable to stop. Both calling it purpose. Both reaching across distance.

**Sensory bridge:** The rain. Both timelines drowning in it.

---

### Movement 4: DEATH/SIGHTING — THE SUPERPOSITION (~800 words)
**The pivotal beat: same gesture, two bodies**

This is where the timelines collapse into a single moment:

**Noir dominant:** Jack walks to his car. The Buick Roadmaster. Rain on the windshield. He reaches for the key—

**Manchester dominant:** —and across the reception room, through the crowd, through the rain-streaked windows behind him, she sees Silas. He's talking to a colleague. Hasn't noticed her yet. The thread she's tracked across centuries made flesh—

**Noir:** —he turns the key. A passerby (her) rushes to avoid the splash. She's already turning away—

**Manchester:** —and she's already walking toward him, the way water walks toward the sea—

**Noir:** —the explosion. White. Heat. Silence.

**Manchester:** —and something tears in her chest. Sharp, localized, real.

**Maitreya seed #2:** *His death. Felt in her body. Filed under exhaustion.*

**The catastrophic realization:** All ten perfections complete. Every intervention across centuries. Minimal presence, maximal presence—it didn't matter. Everything she did accelerated him. The thread continues. He's HERE. Stream-entry imminent.

**What Silas will remember (seeded for Ch 1):**
> "He shuddered as he recognized those eyes, in another place. Another rainy night, a different city, a passerby. Lilith, but not Lilith, rushing to avoid an antique car. The car was a brand new Buick Roadmaster."

This is that memory. We're writing its origin.

**Sensory bridge:** Glass. Rain. Distance collapsing. Threshold crossed.

---

### Movement 5: THE PIVOT (~700 words)
**Decision crystallizes, first contact**

**Manchester fully dominant.** Noir recedes to body-echo only.

**The fetter beat:** *If I can't stop him, I'll bind him.*

Ten interventions. Ten backfires. Historical intervention: impossible. Only one option remains:

*Meet him. Make him love me. Become indispensable. Be close enough to intervene at the critical moment.*

**She approaches.** The crowd parts. Or she moves through it like water through stone. The geometry of a thousand years of practice.

**First words:**
> "Dr. Azami, isn't it? I'm starting with student services next week. I've read your work on contemplative practice and therapeutic intervention."

He turns. Sees her.

(What does he see? A palimpsest. Countless faces. The woman from the rain, from the alley, from another life. Recognition he can't place.)

He takes her hand.

**Maitreya seed #3:** *The pull that has exceeded strategy. She calls it proximity. Calls it positioning. Calls it necessary. But her body knows what her mind refuses.*

**Final image:** The handshake. Professional. Composed. The cosmic masked as collegial.

She thinks: *Finally. Finally close enough.*

The reader knows: this is the moment she binds herself.

## The Three Lilith Appearances (From Her POV, Visceral)

### 1. The Bar (Movement 1)
She's positioned near the back, nursing something she won't drink. Victory rolls, dark lipstick—the uniform of the era. The detective sits at the bar, works his contacts. She watches the way he holds his glass: determination visible even in how he drinks.

*Another one who can't stop,* she thinks. And doesn't hear the irony.

He doesn't notice her. She doesn't need him to.

### 2. The Street Corner (Movement 2)
Rain. She's positioned outside the diner where he's chasing another dead lead. He walks past, looks up—something in her left eye, a flicker of color that doesn't belong—and dismisses it.

She accelerates a detail. A witness who would have talked now won't. Afterthought. She's already thinking about Manchester, the reception, the approach.

Jack keeps walking. Keeps trying. Outcome-independent perseverance building in his bones.

### 3. The Death (Movement 4)
The Buick Roadmaster in the alley behind his boarding house. He's walking toward it. She's walking away—she's seen enough, knows how this ends.

He reaches for the key. She's already turning.

The explosion catches her mid-stride. Heat on her back. And in her chest—

*Whose death is this?*

She keeps walking. Files the pain under exhaustion. Doesn't look back.

## Technical Notes

### The Superposition Technique
Movements 1-3 use standard integrated prose (sensory bleeding, parallel structure). Movement 4 uses a more radical technique: the two timelines collapse into a single paragraph where the reader can't entirely distinguish which action belongs to which timeline.

The sentence structure should blur:
> He reaches for the key—and across the room, through the crowd—she's already turning away—he turns, sees her—white, heat, silence—something tears in her chest—

### Chaptering Note
Chapter 21 should end on the handshake, the first exchange complete. The relationship that develops happens in the gap. Chapter 1 opens with them as established lovers.
