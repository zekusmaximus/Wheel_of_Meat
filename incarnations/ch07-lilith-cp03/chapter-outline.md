## I. The Thread Shifts (~600 words)

**Timeline Balance:** 60/40 Manchester-leading  
**Opens:** Dual sensory anchor—her flat's cold silence / heat arriving before image

### Key Beats:
- Transition from Chandra's bureaucratic thread to Philon's—the flavor changing from ledgers to **skin**
- The plague announces itself through sensation: fever-heat seeping into her flat, the texture of something wrong in the air
- First smell: the sweet-rot that will define this chapter (arrives in her flat as absence-made-presence, the smell of *nothing* that suggests *everything* dying elsewhere)
- Fetter beat begins: analysis of Chandra's backfire, the certainty that refinement will succeed

### Sensory Anchors:
| Manchester | Athens |
|------------|--------|
| Cold tea, radiator clicking | Fever-warmth bleeding through |
| Sterile flat (smell of nothing) | Sweet-rot, pyre-smoke (smell of everything) |
| Silence of chosen isolation | Silence of the dying |

### Meat Moment:
> The thread hummed with a different frequency than Chandra's careful ledgers. This consciousness lived in its hands—hands that could not stop reaching, could not stop touching, even when what they touched was dying.
> 
> She felt the texture before she understood it: rough-smooth, heat radiating, the particular give of flesh that was failing.
> 
> Pustules. The word arrived clinical and distant, but the sensation was neither.

## II. The Dream-Walker (~800 words)

**Timeline Balance:** 50/50 integrated  
**Dominant Moment:** INSIDE the dream-sending (Lilith pressing into Alexios's sleeping mind)

### Key Beats:
- Rumor-acceleration felt as bodies pressing tighter (panic spreading like infection)
- The intimacy of entering a sleeping mind—INSIDE POV as she crafts Alexios's nightmare
- **THE DREAM:** Philon's body on a pyre.The smell of burning mentor. The heat on Alexios's dream-face. She makes him see the meat his teacher will become.
- Alexios wakes carrying the smell. His choice to leave becomes inevitable.
- Lilith's satisfaction—thicker than before, the hunger unnamed

### The Dream (inside POV):
> She pressed into the young man's sleeping consciousness—not taking, not possessing, only *suggesting*—and found the fear-threads waiting.
> 
> What she gave him was simple: Philon on a pyre.
> 
> Not the abstract knowledge of death but its texture. The way fat rendered. The way skin split. The way a body that had been a man—had been *his* man, the one he followed—became meat heating in fire, becoming smoke, becoming nothing.
> 
> The smell stayed longest. She made certain of that. When Alexios woke with his heart pounding and the taste of ash on his tongue, the smell would not leave him.
>  
> He would remember it every time he looked at Philon. He would see the pyre waiting behind the living face.
> 
> *Survival is service,* she whispered at the edge of the dream. *You cannot serve the dead.*

### Sensory Anchors:
| Manchester | Athens |
|------------|--------|
| Her stillness in the chair | Alexios thrashing in sleep |
| The mental intimacy of sending | The smell of burning she places in his mind |
| Satisfaction settling in her chest | The sound of him waking, gasping |

### Meat Moment: The full sensory weight of pyres—fat rendering, skin splitting, meat becoming smoke. She makes Alexios understand that his mentor is meat.

## III. The Compulsion (~700 words)

**Timeline Balance:** 40/60 Historical-leading  
**Key Focus:** The parallel structure becomes visible

### Key Beats:
- Watching Philon reach for the Scythian slave woman at the gate
- His hands on dying flesh—the texture registering across timelines (rough-smooth of pustules, the papery heat of fever-skin)
- The parallel structure: HIS hands reaching / HER awareness reaching—same gesture, same compulsion, same inability to stop
- Recognition of kinship—filed with interest, not alarm
- She sees his mistake clearly: conflating service with suicide
- She does not see that she's doing the same thing

### The Parallel (prose intention):
> Philon knelt beside the slave woman, his hands already reaching. He did not calculate the risk. He did not weigh his life against hers. He simply touched—fever-hot skin, papery texture, the particular roughness of pustules that would never heal.
> 
> The sensation arrived in Lilith's body before her mind could filter it: heat against her palms, something raised under her fingertips, the texture of flesh failing.
> 
> She was reaching too. Not for a dying woman but for the thread—for this consciousness she could not stop tracking, for this pattern she needed to understand.
> 
> His hands on the woman. Her attention on him.
> 
> Both gestures wearing the face of duty.

### Meat Moment: 
- The slave woman's body: young, perhaps thirty, mottled with plague-marks, light as a bird (fever consuming her substance)
- Philon carrying her—the weight of meat that will die in his arms
- The texture of pustules under his physician's hands (and hers, translated through the thread)

## IV. The Wheel Turns (~900 words)

**Timeline Balance:** 80/20 Historical-dominant → surfaces to Manchester  
**Pivotal Moment:** Death and crystallization

### Key Beats:
- The slave woman dies in his arms—heat fading, eyes fixing
- **THE KEY MOMENT:** Her final question, "*Why?*" and his non-answer ("Because you are here")
- His cough begins. The fever recognizes itself.
- **COMPRESSED DEATH:** Key moments only—
  - The heat building behind his eyes
  - The moment he cannot lift the water basin
  - The fever-dreams: the woman from Scythia asking "*Why you? Why waste on one slave?*"
  - The crystallizing question: *Was the one for them—or for me?*
- His body on the pyre (one among twelve, anonymous, meat among meat)
- Lilith surfaces with the kinship-whisper: *We are the same*
- She files it. Returns to analysis. Certainty that refinement will succeed.

### The Death (compressed but visceral):
> The slave woman died three hours before dawn.
> 
> Philon felt her breath stop as a loosening in his own chest—the particular absence where presence had been. Her skin was already cooling, the fever's heat finally released. The pustules that marked her would never heal, would never scar. They would stay exactly as they were, frozen in the moment of almost-breaking.
> 
> He closed her eyes. Arranged her hands on her chest. She had never told him her name.
> 
> And then he coughed.
> 
> ---
> 
> *The next three days compressed into sensation—*
> 
> Heat building behind his eyes. The taste of ash on his tongue (Alexios's dream-taste, though he could not know that). The moment his hands failed to lift a basin of water and he understood.
> 
> He was meat now. Meat that was failing. Meat that would burn.
> 
> *Was the one for them—or for me?*
> 
> The question arrived without answer. He carried it into the darkness with him, a seed that would not sprout in this body.
> 
> ---
> 
> They found him in the evening. They wrapped him without ceremony. They carried him past temples where gods stood in marble silence and placed him on a pyre with eleven others.
> 
> The fire, when it came, was ordinary. Wood and oil. The smell that had become Athens's atmosphere.
> 
> Meat on the wheel.
> 
> The wheel turning.

### Lilith's Surfacing:
> She surfaced gasping—not for air (she did not need air the way others did) but from the weight of proximity. Her hands were cold around ceramic that had gone cold hours ago. The flat held its chosen silence.
> 
> *We are the same.*
> 
> The thought did not frighten her as it had with Ka. It interested her now. Two iterations of the same pattern: the compulsion to reach, the inability to stop, the cost mounting.
> 
> She filed the observation. Returned to analysis.
> 
> *I see what went wrong. I'll correct.*
> 
> Athens still burned in her awareness—pyres and smoke and the question that had crystallized too late to matter. She had accelerated his isolation, sharpened his martyrdom, condensed the lesson into something pure.
> 
> But the lesson had not corrupted. The seed remained viable.
> 
> She would refine. She would adjust.
> 
> The thread hummed forward, and she followed.
