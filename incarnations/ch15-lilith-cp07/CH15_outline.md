## Two Layers of Intervention

### Layer 1: Witnessing Sor Catalina (Past Life)
Lilith can viscerally experience her past life as Sor Catalina, but she does NOT control it and does NOT try to control it. Sor Catalina is who Lilith *was* in that century. When Lilith tracks this life, she re-experiences it from the inside — every sensation, every moment, every choice. But she is *witnessing* her past self, not directing her.

**Sensations to render:**
- Cold stone through sandals, weight of the grey habit
- Incense thick enough to burn the throat
- The conviction that she was doing God's work (because she genuinely felt that then)
- Speaking words without choosing them now — the strange dislocation of memory-as-action

### Layer 2: Pushing Tomás (Active Intervention)
Lilith's TRUE push in this chapter is urging Tomás de Mendoza into betrayal. From her flat in Manchester, she actively amplifies Tomás's fear for his son Rafael, pressing the terror that makes him name Diego to the Inquisition.

**What she pushes:**
- Tomás's terror for Rafael (arrested, vulnerable)
- The calculation that naming Diego might purchase Rafael's safety
- The desperation of a father choosing between friend and son
- The conviction that betrayal is the only path

**The irony:** She believes she is strategically engineering Diego's downfall. But she is also watching herself (as Sor Catalina) participate in that same engineering, centuries ago. The "intervention" is both active (Tomás) and already-completed (Sor Catalina).

### Section 2: The Pattern
**~400 words | Integration: 60/40 Manchester-dominant**

**The race consciousness crystallizes.** Lilith observes the pattern across interventions:

- Each interference has accelerated him
- Each backfire has been structural, not correctable
- The pattern is not failure — it is the nature of opposition itself

**First conscious turn:** "I need to focus on my own escape."

This is not despair — it is strategic recalibration. If opposition always backfires, perhaps the answer is to *outpace* him rather than slow him down.

**Manchester dominant:** Her awareness pulls back from Toledo momentarily. She is in her flat, tracking, and the recognition arrives: *The backfires are structural. Every intervention accelerates him.*

**Toledo as echo:** Sor Catalina's certainty that she is doing God's work — the same certainty Lilith feels about her own interventions. The parallel visible to reader, invisible to her.

**The tragic irony:** She is racing to escape attachment... through attachment. Her final fetter intensifies while she thinks she's addressing it.

### Section 3: The Encounter
**~500 words | Integration: 70/30 Toledo-dominant | Witnessing Past Life**

**The encounter outside San Bartolomé** — Scene 2 of Chapter 14, experienced from inside Sor Catalina.

Lilith re-experiences this moment:
- Standing in the shadow of the church's western wall
- Watching Diego approach — his medical bag against his hip
- Feeling her mouth form words she already spoke: "Physician. You walk late."
- The certainty that filled Sor Catalina — that confession cleanses, that the Holy Office is mercy

**The dislocation:** She is watching herself speak without choosing the words. Memory-as-action. The strange layering of consciousness — present awareness inside past conviction.

**Diego's unease:** She feels his instinctive recoil through her own flesh. The way his eyes slide away from hers. He senses something wrong but cannot name it. She felt nothing wrong at the time. Now, watching herself from within, she notices: *he was afraid of me. And I did not see.*

**Lilith's hands (Manchester):** She reaches for her cold tea and the gesture echoes Sor Catalina reaching toward Diego — "She reached out as though to touch his arm, then stopped."

**No empathy, only observation:** She registers Diego's fear clinically. Does not feel for him. Cannot afford to. (Maitreya hint: the pull she refuses to examine)

### Section 4: The Push
**~400 words | Integration: 40/60 Manchester-dominant | Active Intervention**

**Layer 2: Pushing Tomás.** This is her active intervention — not Sor Catalina's autonomous actions, but Lilith pressing from Manchester.

**What she does:**
- Amplifies Tomás's terror for Rafael
- Makes the calculation feel inevitable: name Diego, save your son
- Presses the desperation until betrayal becomes the only visible path

**Manchester sensations:**
- Her fingers tight around the cold mug
- The flatness of her expression
- The precision of the work — she knows exactly which fears to amplify
- The certainty that this will succeed where other methods failed

**Toledo echoes:**
- Tomás's face (she has seen it across time — the hollow grief, the desperation)
- The weight of what she is doing — no guilt, but awareness of weight
- The belief: *If Diego is betrayed by someone he loves, the hatred will poison him.*

**The strategy:** Personal betrayal is intimate. Diego will hate Tomás specifically, personally. That hatred will be his undoing. This is why she engineers it — not random persecution but *named* betrayal.

**The fetter sharpens:** She is precise. Relentless. The pull she won't name drives the precision. (Fetter beat: "I don't feel what I'm feeling")

### Section 5: The Memory
**~300 words | Integration: 80/20 Manchester-dominant**

**The campus memory arrives.** The claustrophobia of walls closing — flat walls, Toledo walls, cell walls — triggers recall of the university visit (Chapter 13).

**What she remembers:**
- Open spaces. Gothic verticals. Victorian gravity.
- Sunlight on stone (contrast to current grey-on-grey)
- The pull that had drawn her there without knowing why

**The shift:** From past to present. From historical intervention to contemporary possibility.

**The question forms (not yet voiced):** If distance is the problem... if intermediaries always fail... what about direct contact?

**She files it.** Does not examine. Returns to Toledo, to the work. But the seed is planted. The question will grow.

**Manchester sensation:** She realizes her tea has gone cold (again). The flat is too quiet. Too small. Something is changing in her that she refuses to name.

### Section 6: The Chamber
**~300 words | Integration: 70/30 Toledo-dominant | Witnessing Past Life**

**The interrogation chamber** — Scene 6 of Chapter 14, from inside Sor Catalina.

Lilith re-experiences:
- Standing in the corner as "spiritual comfort"
- The candlelight, the scribe's quill, the inquisitors' patient questions
- Diego's eyes sliding toward her, the cold that touches his spine
- Her own stillness — Sor Catalina's certainty that she prays for his soul

**Sustained presence:** She has been watching Diego for weeks (as Sor Catalina). Has counseled Tomás. Has created conditions. Now she watches the machinery consume him.

**The wrongness Diego feels:** She experiences it from the other side. He senses something older than the Inquisition, older than Toledo. He is right. And she did not know, at the time, that he was right. Now she does.

**The investment:** This is deeper than observation. She has *lived* as Sor Catalina. Inhabited this body, spoken these words, believed these convictions. The sustained presence reveals: she has invested more than strategy requires.

**Maitreya hint:** The pull toward him exceeding strategic necessity. She notices how long she has watched. Files it. Continues.

### Section 7: The Fire
**~500 words | Historical DOMINANT | Pivotal Moment**

**The auto-da-fé** — Scene 9 of Chapter 14, from inside Sor Catalina. This is the chapter's pivotal moment where historical timeline dominates.

**Manchester recedes to body-echo:** Grey light on her eyelids. Cold tea forgotten. Her awareness fully in Toledo, in the plaza, in the morning light.

**What Lilith experiences:**
- Standing at the crowd's edge, grey habit, folded hands
- The seven stakes. The sanbenitos. The smell of smoke not yet rising.
- Diego bound, dressed in mockery, his crimes painted on fabric
- Tomás in the crowd — hollow, grey, Rafael beside him. The fruit of her intervention.

**The hatred she expected:**
- Diego should hate. The betrayal was specific. Personal. Intimate.
- Tomás's face is *there*, visible, nameable. The poison should work.

**What happens instead:**
- Diego looks at Tomás. Something flickers in his eyes — the ember of rage.
- Then: release. Diego looks at Tomás and *lets go*. His hands steady. His face calms.
- He does not forgive. But he releases. The coal falls from his hand.

**The backfire crystallizes:**
- She pushed for hatred. He found release.
- She made the betrayal personal so the poison would be personal. He learned that even personal hatred can be set down.
- Her intervention taught him the very lesson she wanted to prevent.

**THE MIRROR MOMENT:**
- Diego's eyes find Sor Catalina in the crowd. Find *her*.
- For a moment — as flames rise, as smoke begins — he looks at her.
- And in his dying eyes, she sees: *her face. The widow's peak. The dark strand falling left. The crescent of green in the brown eye.*
- Her face. Always her face. The same face she has worn across lives she is only now remembering.

**The question surfaces:** "Why is my face always the same?"

**Diego dies.** The flames take him. His final look is not hatred but something stranger — release toward her too. He lets her go.

**She did not want to be released.**

### Section 8: The Seed
**~400 words | Integration: 60/40 Manchester-dominant**

**Manchester returns.** Lilith in her flat. Grey light. Cold tea. The walls too close.

**What she carries:**
- The backfire: personal betrayal failed. Diego released his hatred.
- The face: her face, always the same. Why?
- The question: what if distance is the problem?

**The wheel becomes real:**
- She has felt Sor Catalina's convictions. Inhabited that life.
- She has felt bodies worn and discarded — not as abstract concept but as *memory*.
- The wheel is not metaphor. She has been on it. Is on it still. Has been on it longer than she knew.

**What she perceives (not states):**
- She is close to the threshold. This life, this awareness, this power — culmination.
- So is he. And he's closing faster than expected.
- Every intervention has accelerated him. The pattern is structural.

**The loneliness intensifies:**
- She carries knowledge no one else can understand.
- The wheel revelation makes this worse — she has felt eons of cycling, and there is no one to tell.
- The loneliness is not the kind that breaks her. It is the kind that will eventually drive her to *need* someone to know.

**THE SEED:**
- The question arrives: *What if I met him directly?*
- Not opposition from distance. Not vessels and intermediaries. Not historical intervention.
- Present tense. Face to face. Direct contact.
- The thought is subtle. A question, not a decision. She does not act on it yet.
- But the seed is planted.

**The shift toward Chapter 17 (subtle):**
- Personal betrayal failed. Diego metabolized it into release.
- A half-thought: "Not the goal—the method."
- What if the approach were different? Not feelings but ideas?
- (This seeds Marguerite Viret — philosophical companion — but don't overstate. Leave incomplete.)

**Final beat:**
- She sets down the cold tea. Rises from the tracking corner.
- The flat is still too small. The walls still too close.
- But something has shifted. A direction forming.
- Not yet decision. Just... direction.
