## Core Structural Requirements

### Integrated Prose Technique

This chapter holds Manchester and Rome simultaneously. No alternating scenes. No transition markers. Both timelines exist in every paragraph, connected through:

**Sensory Bridges:**
- Her laptop screen / wax tablets and papyrus
- Cold tea / the warmth of bronze stylus against palm
- The smell of nothing in her flat / the smell of ink, old lamp oil, rancid papyrus
- Silence of her flat / the acoustic weight of contradictory testimony
- The rhythm of tracking notes / the rhythm of investigation

**Procedural Integration:**
- Her investigation of Silas's past lives parallels his investigation of Varro's death
- Contradictions in testimony feel like contradictions in her strategy
- The "case" she's building against him has the same structure as the case he's building
- Evidence refuses to cohere in both timelines

## Intervention Architecture

### The Three Interferences

**Interference #1 — The Scribal Error (Scene 2)**
- Method: Induces brief distraction in clerk copying testimony
- Effect: "Intemperate" becomes "temperate"—meaning inverts
- Function: Seeds cognitive fracture; creates parallel evidential records
- Her feeling: Satisfaction at introducing microscopic chaos

**Interference #2 — The Traffic Delay (Scene 4)**
- Method: Triggers noisy argument between butcher and merchant
- Effect: Key witness arrives late; earlier alibi becomes impossible
- Function: Timeline collapses—forces Verinus to choose what to believe
- Her feeling: Deeper investment; the manipulations require more focus

**Interference #3 — The Senator's Rage (Scene 4)**
- Method: Amplifies senator's displeasure into disproportionate rage
- Effect: Verinus exiled for choosing compassion over compromise
- Intent: Strand him in meaninglessness—destroy a man who needed coherence
- Actual result: Exile gives him what Rome never could: permission to not know

### The Catastrophic Backfire

**What she intended:** The accumulated contradictions should make Verinus cynical. A man who cannot trust evidence should trust power. He should sacrifice the innocent servant to preserve his position.

**What happened:** The contradictions didn't make him cynical—they made him humble. He couldn't know with certainty, so he chose with compassion. The exile she engineered freed him from Rome's certainty-obsessed culture.

**Her realization (suppressed):** "I gave him silence. I gave him time. I gave him permission to not know."

- The senator's temper—a thread she can pull

**Prose Beat 3:** The Choice (HISTORICAL DOMINANT)
