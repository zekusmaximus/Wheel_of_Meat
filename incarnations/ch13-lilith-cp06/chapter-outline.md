## Core Structural Requirements

### Integrated Prose Technique

This chapter holds Manchester and Heian simultaneously. No alternating scenes. No transition markers. Both timelines exist in every paragraph, connected through:

**Sensory Bridges:**
- Manchester autumn light ↔ Heian moonlight (both filtered, both insufficient)
- Her flat's stripped walls ↔ The Attendant's perfectly preserved screens (both artificial enclosures)
- Walking to university ↔ The Attendant walking to Lady Hotaru's pavilion (both drawn, neither understanding why)
- Cold ceramic warming in hands ↔ Smooth lacquer warming from proximity (objects registering temperature against will)
- November air on skin ↔ Perfumed evening air on skin (both bodies noticing sensation)

**Aesthetic Integration:**
- Beautiful things noticed everywhere (prose registers beauty, she doesn't comment)
- Longing as texture, not stated emotion
- The pull toward form—objects, light, architecture
- Both timelines saturated with visual and tactile precision
- *Mono no aware* in contemporary register: everything passes, beauty included

## Intervention Architecture

### The Attendant Strategy

In Chapter 12, Lilith manifested as The Attendant—a servant woman in Lady Hotaru's household. This was different from previous manifestations:

**What Made It Different:**
- First physical appearance to him as *object of desire* (not authority, not distant)
- Intimate proximity: scrubbing floors in his presence, eye contact, physical closeness
- The Attendant's obsessive maintenance: scrubbing hands raw, bleaching, burying autumn leaves
- She chose to inhabit a servant's body—to be *near*, not above

### The Backfire

**What she intended:** Create perfect frozen beauty to trap Kaoru in the realm of desire. Make him so attached to preserved spring that he never transcends aesthetic longing.

**What happened:** Static perfection *repelled* rather than bound. Kaoru learned that beauty requires impermanence—"beauty was something you witnessed... It required impermanence." The trap became the teacher. His death-poem: "To endure what is real. That is enough. That is everything."

**Her realization (suppressed):** This backfire is strangest yet. She gave him *exactly* what he wanted—frozen beauty, eternal spring—and it freed him. The perfection she arranged was the cure for the illness she was feeding.

## What Holds Her Back: The Proximity Aftereffect

The Attendant manifestation left residue she won't examine:

**The Core Mechanic:** Proximity through embodiment creates different knowledge than tracking:
- She was *close*—close enough to touch, to be looked at, to register in his awareness
- The Attendant-vessel felt things: the weight of lacquer, the texture of silk, the warmth of standing near him
- Her hands ached from scrubbing (not her hands—the vessel's—but she felt them)
- He looked at the Attendant and almost recognized something. She felt him *seeing*.

**The Effect:**
- Something about being *present* that distance-manipulation doesn't provide
- The intimacy of opposition (face-to-face across time)
- A flicker of what it might mean to exist *with* him rather than *against* him
- SEED FOR CH 21: closeness works differently than distance

**The Suppression:**
Each time this surfaces, she files it:
- "Vessel mechanics create perception noise" (blame the technique)
- "Prolonged manifestation left karmic residue" (blame the process)  
- "The pull toward him is tactical" (rename the feeling as strategy)
