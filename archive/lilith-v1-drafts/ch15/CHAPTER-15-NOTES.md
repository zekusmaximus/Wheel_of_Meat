## Core Function

This chapter reveals the supernatural frame absent from Chapter 14. Readers saw Sor Catalina watching Diego from corners, counseling Tomás, attending the interrogation—but did not know she was Lilith's vessel. Now we see Lilith's POV:

1. **The Intervention Explained:** How she found and inhabited Sor Catalina; what she attempted
2. **The Backfire Witnessed:** Diego's release of hatred despite (because of) the crucible she created
3. **The Discovery:** She learns the "one Buddha per world-system" rule—raising the stakes catastrophically
4. **The Demonstration:** She shows mastery of Patience (khanti)—the very fetter Diego is learning
5. **The Doubled Irony:** She can release ill-will perfectly in her own life but couldn't see her intervention would teach Diego the same

## Continuity from Chapter 14

### What Lilith Did (As Seen Through Diego's POV)

1. **Inhabited Sor Catalina** - beata (lay holy woman) in Toledo, known for visions and counsel
2. **Counseled Tomás de Mendoza** - guided him toward confession after his son Rafael was arrested
3. **Never spoke directly to Diego** - only watched from shadows, corners, interrogation chamber
4. **Created the conditions** - Tomás's testimony felt necessary; the betrayal became specific and personal
5. **Attended the auto-de-fé** - witnessed Diego's death, release of hatred, peaceful burning

### The Backfire

By making the betrayal personal (Tomás, not abstract persecution), Lilith ensured Diego had to confront **specific hatred** toward a named face. Abstract hatred is easy to suppress. Hatred toward the man who taught you prayers and sold you out requires genuine spiritual work. The specificity became the crucible. Diego released ill-will completely because the test was so difficult it forced true transformation.

## Chapter Structure & Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: Demonstration—Patience in Practice (~1,200 words)

**Setting:** Lilith's office, Manchester, morning

**Function:** Establish Patience demonstration before revealing tracking session

**Beats:**
- Patient arrives late (third time), defensive, making excuses
- Lilith navigates the encounter with perfect patience—no irritation, no judgment
- Patient describes conflict with boss who keeps changing expectations
- Lilith demonstrates sustainable patience: "Patience isn't enduring what we must. It's releasing the need for things to be different than they are."
- Physical tell: **Her hands tighten briefly on the armrest**—she's tracking tonight, the pull is there
- Patient thanks her; she's helped him see his boss differently
- After patient leaves, Lilith sits in silence—the irony approaching but not yet visible

**Demonstration Completed:** She shows mastery of Patience. The physical tell (hands tightening) undercuts her perfect equanimity—she's eager to track, to intervene, to force outcomes rather than accept them.

**Voice:** Clinical precision, Manchester rain outside, undermining clause present

### Scene 3: The Tracking Session—Inhabiting Sor Catalina (~2,000 words) **[CLIMAX]**

**Setting:** Lilith's flat, night, Manchester

**Function:** Reveal the supernatural frame; show what Diego couldn't see

**Part A: Entry and Inhabitation**

- Lilith settles into tracking posture—familiar ritual now
- Reaches back to Toledo, 1491—the converso physician she's been following
- **New technique:** Sustaining presence requires finding a vessel
- Scans the period for her own past lives—finds Sor Catalina, a beata known for visions
- The vessel is perfect: respected, has access, already drawn to theological questions
- Lilith pushes into Sor Catalina's consciousness—not full control, but sustained guidance
- **Key constraint shown:** Sor Catalina acts from her own psychology; Lilith can only amplify, suggest, create conditions
- The vessel genuinely believes her visions; genuinely believes confession cleanses

**Part B: The Intervention Through Sor Catalina's Actions**

- Lilith (through Sor Catalina) counsels Tomás after Rafael's arrest
- Amplifies Tomás's fear for his son—makes the choice feel inevitable
- Guides him toward testimony: "The Holy Office extends mercy to those who confess fully"
- Watches through Sor Catalina's eyes as Tomás visits Diego in prison—the betrayal revealed
- Present in interrogation chamber—watches Diego's face, measuring the hatred growing
- **The strategy:** Cultivate his hatred until ill-will becomes an unbreakable fetter

**Part C: The Auto-de-Fé**

- Lilith (as Sor Catalina) attends the burning—ostensibly to pray for souls
- Watches Diego at the stake, expecting to see hatred consuming him
- Instead: **She sees him release it**
- The recognition moment: "He set it down. After everything I did to him, he *set it down.*"
- The backfire complete: suffering became crucible for releasing ill-will, not cultivating it

**Part D: Withdrawal**

- Lilith pulls out of the vessel—exhausted, disoriented
- Returns to Manchester flat, body trembling from the sustained presence
- Physical cost: hours lost, headache, the sense of having been someone else
- Sits in darkness cataloging the failure

**Voice Notes:**
- Tracking described with clinical precision
- Sor Catalina's perspective shown but clearly pushed by Lilith
- Diego's release of hatred witnessed from outside—no access to his interiority
- Undermining throughout: "genuine belief—and Lilith's guidance beneath it"
